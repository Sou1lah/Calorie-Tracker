05 The Rules of Bulking
01
Protein is non-negotiable
Hit 1.6–2.2g per kg of bodyweight every single day. Without enough protein, extra calories just become fat — your body can't build muscle from carbs and fats alone.
02
Small surplus wins long-term
Your body can build roughly 0.25–0.5kg of muscle per week max. Any calories beyond what's needed for this growth gets stored as fat. Stay at +200–500 kcal.
03
Carbs fuel your training
Muscle glycogen is your primary training fuel. Low carbs = worse workouts = less stimulus = less muscle. Time the bulk of your carbs around your training sessions.
04
Sleep is when you actually grow
Peak growth hormone and testosterone release happens during deep sleep. Muscle protein synthesis runs highest overnight. 7–9 hours is not optional — it's part of the program.
05
Track the scale weekly, not daily
Daily weight fluctuates by 1–2kg from water, food, and hormones. Take a 7-day average. Target 0.25–0.5kg/week gained. Faster = too much fat. Slower = increase calories by 100–150.
06
Progressive overload is the real driver
Calories create the environment. But you still have to give your body a reason to build muscle. If you're not lifting heavier or doing more reps over time, no amount of food will build mass.
